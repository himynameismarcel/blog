About two years ago I had decided to finally start my own blog. I knew that I wanted to use a static site generator (with its obvious advantages and easy maintainability) and so after researching for various alternatives and having narrowed it down to the choice between pelikan and jekyll and their easy compatibility with github (pages), I ultimately decided to use jekyll. While I did have some background in html, css and javascript, I remember that it did take me a few days to finally set up my blog with jekyll. While the resources are plenty (I actually should have written a dedicated blog-post back then about how I set up my blog to remember exactly everything), I do remember that the video series on Jekyll provided by the guys from Giraffe Academy had helped me the most. I hosted the final result on github which powered my final web-page at himynameismarcel.github.io.
